All contributors to HEBBS must sign a Contributor License Agreement (CLA) before their first contribution can be merged. This is a standard practice for open-source projects that ensures the project maintainers have the necessary rights to distribute contributions.

## Why a CLA?

The CLA serves several purposes:

- **Legal clarity** — it establishes that you have the right to contribute the code you're submitting.
- **License compatibility** — it ensures all contributions are compatible with the project's BSL 1.1 license.
- **Patent protection** — it includes a patent grant that protects both the project and its users.
- **Attribution** — it confirms that you are the original author (or have authorization from the original author).

## What the CLA Covers

By signing the CLA, you agree that:

1. You have the legal right to make the contribution.
2. You grant the project a perpetual, worldwide, non-exclusive, royalty-free license to use, reproduce, and distribute your contribution.
3. You grant a patent license for any patents that cover your contribution.
4. You are not expected to provide support for your contribution (though it is appreciated).
5. Your contribution is provided "as is" without warranty.

## How to Sign

### Individual Contributors

1. Open your first pull request.
2. The CLA bot will automatically comment with a link to the CLA.
3. Read the full CLA text and sign electronically.
4. The bot will update the PR status once your signature is recorded.

### Corporate Contributors

If you are contributing on behalf of your employer:

1. Download the Corporate CLA from the repository (`CLA-corporate.md`).
2. Have an authorized representative of your organization sign it.
3. Email the signed CLA to `cla@hebbs.ai`.
4. Once processed, all employees of your organization are covered.

## Full CLA Text

The complete CLA text is available in the repository at [`CLA.md`](https://github.com/hebbs-ai/hebbs/blob/main/CLA.md).

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Do I need to sign the CLA for every contribution?**
No. You sign once and it covers all future contributions to the project.

**Q: Does the CLA transfer ownership of my code?**
No. You retain full ownership and copyright of your contributions. The CLA grants a license, not ownership.

**Q: What if my employer owns my code?**
If your employment agreement assigns IP to your employer, your employer should sign the Corporate CLA.

**Q: Can I still use my contributed code in other projects?**
Yes. You retain all rights to your code. The CLA is a non-exclusive license.

## Questions

If you have questions about the CLA, please open an issue on GitHub or email `legal@hebbs.ai`.
